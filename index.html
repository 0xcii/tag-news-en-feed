Bridge has experienced remarkable growth, reporting a tenfold increase in business this year alone. CEO Zach Abrams noted the company's rapid expansion and its existing clientele, which includes notable firms like Coinbase and SpaceX. Bridge will reportedly serve as a Web3 version of Stripe, enabling seamless payment solutions for digital transactions worldwide. The acquisition comes at a time when digital currencies are gaining traction, largely due to recent decisions by the US Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) that have eased regulations surrounding cryptocurrencies. This shift has encouraged major companies, including PayPal, to embrace digital currencies. PayPal launched its own US dollar stablecoin last year. International Transactions Although the details about the timeline for the acquisition were not disclosed, the move could position Stripe to compete effectively in the digital finance space.
